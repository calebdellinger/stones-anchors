Resilience Stands Back Up

Setbacks do not disqualify a leader. They reveal whether he can rise again.
Leadership principle
Plans fall apart. Pressure increases. Deadlines move. A resilient leader becomes the calm in the storm. He keeps his footing, regains momentum, and helps the people around him recover instead of collapse.
Resilience is not pretending nothing happened. It is absorbing the hit without surrendering the mission.
Verses
Proverbs 24:16
The righteous fall seven times and rise again.
2 Corinthians 4:8–9
We are afflicted in every way, but not crushed; struck down, but not destroyed.
Galatians 6:9
Do not grow weary of doing good, for in due season we will reap.
Main takeaway
The leader's steadiness in crisis often becomes the team's stability.
