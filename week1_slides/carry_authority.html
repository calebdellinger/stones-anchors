Servant · Subsection 2

Servants Carry the Master's Authority

A trusted servant does not merely represent the master's words. He carries the weight of the master's name and moves in the master's authority.
What this means
In biblical leadership, speaking on God's behalf is only half the picture. Servants are also called to obey, act, and move in faith. Boldness is not self-generated swagger; it is the confidence that comes from carrying the assignment of the One who sent you.
Faith becomes visible when it moves. A man may claim belief all day, but leadership begins when belief is translated into obedient action.
Verses

2 Timothy 1:7
God has not given us a spirit of fear, but of power, love, and a sound mind.

James 2:17
Faith without works is dead.

John 20:21
As the Father has sent Me, even so I am sending you.

Acts 4:29
Grant Your servants to continue to speak Your word with all boldness.

Main takeaway
Biblical authority is never passive. It speaks, acts, and obeys.
