Servant · Subsection 1

Servants Belong to the House

A biblical servant is not an isolated worker. He lives from the household of the Master.
Identity, provision, and covering are tied to whose house he belongs to, not to how independent he appears.
What this means
In the ancient world, a servant was part of the master's oikos, or household. He was shaped by the order of that house, protected by its reputation, and sustained by its resources. Servanthood began with belonging before it moved into task.
New Testament anchors

Ephesians 2:19
You are no longer strangers, but members of the household of God.

Galatians 4:7
In Christ, you are no longer a slave, but a son and heir.

Study takeaway: Kingdom leadership starts by settling the question of ownership. Before a man leads well, he must know whose house he is under and whose provision he trusts.
