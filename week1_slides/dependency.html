Servant · Subsection 3

Dependency Beats Autonomy

Surrendered will
The core of biblical servanthood is not simply doing assigned tasks. It is yielding personal rule. A servant brings his desires, plans, and instincts into alignment with the purpose of the Master.

John 15:5
Apart from Me, you can do nothing.

Romans 12:1
Present your bodies as a living sacrifice to God.

Here I am
Hineni

A posture of being available, attentive, and ready before God.

Leadership takeaway
Biblical leadership is not radical self-direction. It is radical availability to God. The strongest men are not those who insist on autonomy, but those who can say yes to God's direction quickly, fully, and without bargaining.

Galatians 2:20
It is no longer I who live, but Christ who lives in me.

Philippians 2:13
God works in you both to will and to work for His good pleasure.
