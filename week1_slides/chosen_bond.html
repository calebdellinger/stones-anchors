Servant · Subsection 4

Devotion Is a Chosen Bond

The bondservant picture says devotion can become a willing bond, not merely an imposed duty.

Leadership under Jesus is a conscious choice to let Him write the story, direct the house, and lead us from glory unto even greater glory. What we surrender to Him is never wasted; it becomes seed in a kingdom economy that outlasts self-made ambition.

Verses

2 Corinthians 9:6–7
Whoever sows sparingly reaps sparingly, and whoever sows bountifully reaps bountifully. God loves a cheerful giver.

Galatians 6:7–9
Whatever a man sows, that will he also reap. Do not grow weary in doing good.

Romans 12:11
Do not be slothful in zeal, but be fervent in spirit, serving the Lord.

Colossians 3:23–24
Work heartily, as for the Lord and not for men, knowing you are serving the Lord Christ.

Main takeaway: Devotion is costly, but the return on surrender is greater than anything a self-directed life can produce. Servanthood chooses the Master's house because it trusts the Master's character.
