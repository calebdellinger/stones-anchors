Delegation Multiplies the Mission

A boss controls people. A leader develops people and entrusts real responsibility.

Delegation is not a way to avoid work. It is how leadership scales the mission, prevents burnout, and trains the next generation of able men to carry meaningful weight.

Biblical pattern
Exodus 18:21
Jethro tells Moses to appoint able men over groups of thousands, hundreds, fifties, and tens.
Ecclesiastes 4:12
Though one may be overpowered, two can defend themselves. A cord of three strands is not quickly broken.
Ephesians 4:11–12
Leaders are given to equip the saints for the work of ministry.
1 Corinthians 12:7
To each is given the manifestation of the Spirit for the common good.
The point is not mere efficiency. It is shared strength. Authority entrusted wisely creates health in both the mission and the men carrying it.
When leadership gives others room to own real tasks, the work expands, strength is reinforced, and future leaders are formed in the process.
