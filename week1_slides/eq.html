Emotional Intelligence Guards Relationships

Self-awareness
Leaders must know their own triggers, blind spots, and patterns. Men who cannot read themselves usually misread everyone else too.
James 1:19
Be quick to hear, slow to speak, and slow to anger.
Galatians 5:22–23
The fruit of the Spirit includes self-control, patience, and gentleness.
Leadership is not only direction. It is the stewardship of people.

Emotional intelligence helps a leader read the room, understand what motivates men differently, and choose a tone that brings clarity instead of unnecessary heat.

Relational impact
Empathy is not softness. It is the disciplined ability to respond in a way that protects the mission and the relationship at the same time.
Proverbs 15:1
A soft answer turns away wrath, but harsh words stir up anger.
Colossians 4:6
Let your speech always be gracious, seasoned with salt.
Philippians 2:4
Look not only to your own interests, but also to the interests of others.
