# Week 2: Obedience - The True Proof of Faith

## Slide 1: The Ultimate Test of Obedience
**Title:** The Ultimate Test of Obedience
**Content:** True obedience is tested not in the easy commands, but in the ones that cost us everything. The story of Abraham and Isaac stands as the pinnacle of biblical obedience. God asked Abraham to sacrifice the very promise He had given him. Abraham's response was immediate and unwavering action, demonstrating that his trust in the Promiser was greater than his attachment to the promise itself.

*   Genesis 22:1: "Some time later God tested Abraham and said to him, 'Abraham!' 'Here I am,' he answered."
*   Genesis 22:2: "Take your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to the land of Moriah. Offer him there as a burnt offering on one of the mountains, which I will show you."
*   Genesis 22:3: "Early the next morning Abraham got up and loaded his donkey. He took with him two of his servants and his son Isaac. When he had cut wood for the burnt offering, he set out for the place God had told him about."
*   **Context:** In the ancient Near East, child sacrifice was a known practice among surrounding nations, but abhorrent to Yahweh. This test was designed to prove Abraham's ultimate allegiance and to definitively separate Yahweh's requirements from those of pagan deities, culminating in the provision of a substitute.

## Slide 2: Obedience is Better Than Sacrifice
**Title:** Obedience is Better Than Sacrifice
**Content:** We often try to substitute religious activity for actual obedience. King Saul learned the hard way that God desires our yielded hearts more than our religious performances. When we disobey God's clear instructions but try to cover it up with "good works" or "sacrifices," we miss the heart of God entirely. True worship is simply doing what God asked us to do.

*   1 Samuel 15:21: "The soldiers took sheep and cattle from the plunder, the best of what was devoted to God, in order to sacrifice them to the Lord your God at Gilgal."
*   1 Samuel 15:22: "But Samuel replied: 'Does the Lord delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices as much as in obeying the Lord? To obey is better than sacrifice, and to heed is better than the fat of rams.'"
*   1 Samuel 15:23: "For rebellion is like the sin of divination, and arrogance like the evil of idolatry. Because you have rejected the word of the Lord, he has rejected you as king."
*   **Context:** Saul had been commanded to completely destroy the Amalekites, but he kept the best livestock, claiming it was for a sacrifice to God. Samuel's rebuke establishes a fundamental biblical principle: external religious rituals are meaningless and even offensive to God if they are accompanied by a rebellious and disobedient heart.

## Slide 3: The Faith Behind the Action
**Title:** The Faith Behind the Action
**Content:** Obedience is never blind; it is rooted in a deep trust in the character of God. Abraham could obey the agonizing command to sacrifice Isaac because he believed that God was able to raise the dead. Our obedience is directly proportional to our faith. When we waver in our obedience, it is usually because we have wavered in our belief that God is good and capable of fulfilling His promises.

*   Hebrews 11:16: "Instead, they were longing for a better country—a heavenly one. Therefore God is not ashamed to be called their God, for he has prepared a city for them."
*   Hebrews 11:17: "By faith Abraham, when God tested him, offered Isaac as a sacrifice. He who had embraced the promises was about to sacrifice his one and only son,"
*   Hebrews 11:18: "even though God had said to him, 'It is through Isaac that your offspring will be reckoned.'"
*   **Context:** The author of Hebrews highlights that Abraham's obedience was not a mindless resignation to fate, but a calculated faith. He reasoned that God's promise regarding Isaac's lineage was unbreakable, leading to the logical conclusion that God would have to resurrect Isaac if the sacrifice was carried out.

## Slide 4: The Ultimate Example of Obedience
**Title:** The Ultimate Example of Obedience
**Content:** Abraham's obedience points us to the ultimate obedience of Jesus Christ. While Abraham was spared from sacrificing his son, God the Father did not spare His own. Jesus modeled perfect obedience by willingly stepping down from glory and submitting to the cross. His obedience reversed the curse of Adam's disobedience and paved the way for our salvation.

*   Philippians 2:7: "rather, he made himself nothing by taking the very nature of a servant, being made in human likeness."
*   Philippians 2:8: "And being found in appearance as a man, he humbled himself by becoming obedient to death—even death on a cross!"
*   Philippians 2:9: "Therefore God exalted him to the highest place and gave him the name that is above every name,"
*   **Context:** Paul uses an early Christian hymn to illustrate the incarnation and crucifixion. The contrast is stark: whereas Adam grasped at equality with God and disobeyed, bringing death, Christ relinquished His rights and obeyed to the point of a shameful execution, bringing life and exaltation.
