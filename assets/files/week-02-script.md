## Slide 1: Cover (Obedience)
*(Slide is up as men are taking their seats. Welcome everyone and transition into the topic.)*

**Opening:**
Welcome back to week two. Last week we looked at Servant Leadership — the idea that biblical authority is always paired with humility. Tonight, we are looking at the foundation of that humility: Obedience.

Andrew Murray once wrote, "The only proof of true faith is obedience." That is a heavy statement. It means that what we *say* we believe is ultimately proven by what we *do* when God speaks. Tonight, we are going to look at the anatomy of obedience. What does it actually look like when it costs us something? To do that, we are going to anchor our time in one of the most intense, demanding stories in all of Scripture: Genesis 22.

## Slide 2: The Ultimate Test — Genesis 22
*(Advance to Slide 2)*

**Teaching:**
If you want to know what obedience looks like, you have to look at a command that costs you everything. In Genesis 22, God asks Abraham to sacrifice Isaac. Understand the weight of this: Isaac is not just a son. He is the child of the covenant. He is the *promise*.

God is testing Abraham. And the test is this: Do you trust the Promiser more than you trust the promise? Do you love the Giver more than the gift?

Look at Abraham's response. There is no argument recorded. There is no delay. Genesis 22:3 says he rose *early the next morning*, saddled his donkey, and went. He had three days of walking toward Mount Moriah. Three days to talk himself out of it. Three days to turn back. He never does. This is the anatomy of true obedience: hearing clearly, moving immediately, and trusting completely.

*   **Genesis 22:1** — "Some time later God tested Abraham and said to him, 'Abraham!' 'Here I am,' he answered."
*   **Genesis 22:2** — "Take your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to the land of Moriah. Offer him there as a burnt offering on one of the mountains, which I will show you."
*   **Genesis 22:3** — "Early the next morning Abraham got up and loaded his donkey. He took with him two of his servants and his son Isaac. When he had cut wood for the burnt offering, he set out for the place God had told him about."
    *   *Context:* In the ancient Near East, child sacrifice was a known practice among surrounding nations, but abhorrent to Yahweh. This test was designed to prove Abraham's ultimate allegiance and to definitively separate Yahweh's requirements from those of pagan deities, culminating in the provision of a substitute.

**Discussion Prompt:**
Where in your life are you currently tempted to hold onto the "promise" or the "gift" tighter than you are holding onto God Himself?

## Slide 3: To Obey Is Better Than Sacrifice
*(Advance to Slide 3)*

**Teaching:**
Abraham shows us immediate obedience. King Saul shows us the exact opposite. In 1 Samuel 15, God gives Saul a clear command: completely destroy the Amalekites. Saul disobeys. He keeps the best of the livestock, but he tries to dress his disobedience up in religious language. He tells Samuel, "I kept these to sacrifice to the Lord!"

Samuel's rebuke cuts right through the religious performance. He says, "To obey is better than sacrifice." God does not want your religious activity if your heart is rebellious. Saul had the right religion, but the wrong obedience.

Why did Abraham obey while Saul failed? The writer of Hebrews tells us in chapter 11. Abraham's obedience was grounded in faith. He believed God could raise the dead. Abraham obeyed because he knew who God was. Saul disobeyed because he trusted his own judgment over God's command.

*   **1 Samuel 15:21** — "The soldiers took sheep and cattle from the plunder, the best of what was devoted to God, in order to sacrifice them to the Lord your God at Gilgal."
*   **1 Samuel 15:22** — "But Samuel replied: 'Does the Lord delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices as much as in obeying the Lord? To obey is better than sacrifice, and to heed is better than the fat of rams.'"
*   **1 Samuel 15:23** — "For rebellion is like the sin of divination, and arrogance like the evil of idolatry. Because you have rejected the word of the Lord, he has rejected you as king."
    *   *Context:* Saul had been commanded to completely destroy the Amalekites, but he kept the best livestock, claiming it was for a sacrifice to God. Samuel's rebuke establishes a fundamental biblical principle: external religious rituals are meaningless and even offensive to God if they are accompanied by a rebellious and disobedient heart.

**Discussion Prompt:**
In what ways do we try to substitute "religious activity" (going to church, serving, giving) for actual obedience in the areas where God has asked us to change or surrender?

*(Transition: Abraham's obedience was incredible, but it was only a shadow of what was to come.)*

## Slide 4: The Ultimate Obedience — Christ
*(Advance to Slide 4)*

**Teaching:**
Abraham's obedience on Mount Moriah is a shadow. Christ's obedience on Calvary is the substance.

When Abraham raised the knife, God stopped his hand and provided a ram. But centuries later, the Father gave what He asked Abraham to give. And this time, He did not stop His own hand. There was no substitute for the Son.

Philippians 2 tells us that Jesus humbled Himself, becoming obedient to the point of death—even death on a cross. Adam grasped for equality with God in a garden, disobeyed, and brought death. Jesus surrendered His rights, obeyed to the point of death, and brought life.

We don't obey just to be good men. We obey because Christ obeyed first. His obedience is our model, and because of Romans 5:19—by His obedience we are made righteous—His obedience is our salvation.

*   **Philippians 2:7** — "rather, he made himself nothing by taking the very nature of a servant, being made in human likeness."
*   **Philippians 2:8** — "And being found in appearance as a man, he humbled himself by becoming obedient to death—even death on a cross!"
*   **Philippians 2:9** — "Therefore God exalted him to the highest place and gave him the name that is above every name,"
    *   *Context:* Paul uses an early Christian hymn to illustrate the incarnation and crucifixion. The contrast is stark: whereas Adam grasped at equality with God and disobeyed, bringing death, Christ relinquished His rights and obeyed to the point of a shameful execution, bringing life and exaltation.

**Closing Challenge:**
Obedience is the proof of faith. Where is God calling you to step out in faith this week, drop the negotiations, and simply say "Yes"?
