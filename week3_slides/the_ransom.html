The Ransom — Why We Can Sustain Sacrifice

The Root
We cannot sustain sacrifice by willpower. We need a source.
Men who sacrifice out of duty eventually burn out and grow bitter. The only sustainable source of sacrificial love is a constant return to the One who sacrificed Himself for us. Jesus did not model sacrifice and then leave us to figure it out. He paid the ransom and then gave us His Spirit to live it out.
1
Christ paid the ransom — He came not to be served, but to serve and give His life. (Mark 10:45)
2
We receive the mercy — All of God's compassion and grace poured out on us. (Romans 11:36)
3
We offer our lives — As a living sacrifice, not to earn favor, but as a logical response to grace. (Romans 12:1)
4
The cycle continues — Sacrifice at home, at work, in the community — fueled by the gospel, not by guilt.
The Challenge
Sacrifice is the currency of the kingdom. Where is God asking you to spend it this week?
Mark 10:45 (ESV)
"For even the Son of Man came not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many."
Greek lutron — the price paid to purchase a slave's freedom. Jesus redefines greatness in the same breath: true leadership is measured not by how many serve you, but by how many you serve. This verse is the theological engine of all Christian sacrifice.
Romans 12:1 (ESV)
"I appeal to you therefore, brothers, by the mercies of God, to present your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy and acceptable to God, which is your spiritual worship."
After 11 chapters of the gospel, Paul turns to application. A "living sacrifice" is an oxymoron — sacrifices die. But God wants us alive, continually offering our daily actions to Him. The word "therefore" connects everything: we sacrifice because of the mercy we have received.
John 15:13 (ESV)
"Greater love has no one than this, that someone lay down his life for his friends."
Jesus says this hours before the cross. He is not describing an abstract principle — He is announcing what He is about to do. Every act of sacrifice we make as husbands, fathers, and leaders is a small echo of this defining moment.
Sacrifice is not the price of discipleship. It is the proof that the gospel has actually taken root in us.
