Sacrifice · The Husband & Father

Love Her As Christ Loved the Church — Ephesians 5:25–26

The Standard
Your primary job is to lay down your life so your family can flourish.
Paul gives husbands the highest, most demanding standard in the entire Bible. You don't sacrifice for your family to earn their love. You sacrifice because Christ first sacrificed for you. The goal is not a happy home — it is a holy one.
As Husband
Die to your ego — absorb the hit, initiate the apology, lead in repentance.
As Father
Give your presence, not just your provision. Your time is the sacrifice.
As Leader
Take responsibility for the spiritual climate of your home. You set the tone.
Reflect: What is one specific area in your marriage or parenting where you need to "give yourself up" this week for the flourishing of your family?
Ephesians 5:25 (ESV)
"Husbands, love your wives, as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her,"
Greek agapate — the command is in the present active imperative: keep on loving, continuously. In the Greco-Roman world, husbands had absolute authority and virtually no commanded duties toward their wives. Paul radically subverts this by commanding husbands to imitate the cross.
Ephesians 5:26 (ESV)
"that he might sanctify her, having cleansed her by the washing of water with the word,"
The purpose of Christ's sacrifice was her sanctification — her becoming fully who she was made to be. A sacrificial husband takes responsibility not just for his wife's happiness, but for her holiness and flourishing.
The Model & The Motivation
We sacrifice for our families not to earn love, but because we have already received it. Christ's sacrifice is both the pattern we follow and the power that makes it possible.
