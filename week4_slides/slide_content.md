## Slide 2 — "Passion Begins with a Definition, Not a Feeling"

**Type:** Two-column content slide
**Eyebrow:** Passion · The Language of Fire
**Title:** Passion Begins with a Definition, Not a Feeling

**Left column — The Story:**
- Section label: The Root
- Story title: What the Bible Actually Means by "Passion"
- Body: The English word "passion" has been domesticated into a synonym for enthusiasm. But its theological root is *pathos* — Greek for suffering. The "Passion of Christ" is not about His feelings. It is about His death.
- Body: Three Hebrew and Greek words define Biblical passion: *Qin'ah* (jealous zeal — the fire that will not share what it loves), *Zeo* (to boil — not warm enthusiasm but rolling, overflowing intensity), and *Esh* (fire — the 370-times-used signature of God's presence, judgment, and love).
- Body: The pattern is consistent: passion in Scripture is not what you feel. It is what you are willing to suffer for. It is measured not by emotion but by cost.
- Takeaway: The question is not "How passionate am I?" The question is: "What am I willing to burn for?"

**Right column — Key verses:**
- Deuteronomy 4:24: "For the LORD your God is a consuming fire, a jealous God." — God's first self-description using fire language is not about warmth. It is about exclusive, consuming devotion.
- Romans 12:11: "Do not be slothful in zeal, be fervent in spirit, serve the Lord." — *Zeo* = to boil. Paul commands a boiling intensity, not a comfortable warmth.
- Acts 1:3: "He presented himself alive to them after his suffering…" — The Greek *pathos* (suffering) is the root of our word "passion." The Passion of Christ is the supreme definition: love that absorbs the full cost of what it loves.

## Slide 3 — "The Fire Has Always Been There — From Eden to Pentecost"

**Type:** Two-column content slide
**Eyebrow:** Passion · The Canonical Arc
**Title:** The Fire Has Always Been There — From Eden to Pentecost

**Left column — The Story:**
- Section label: The Arc
- Story title: Seven Moments When God Showed Up as Fire
- Body: The Bible is not a book that occasionally mentions fire. Fire is God's recurring signature — from the first chapter of human history to the last. It appears at every major turning point.
- Body: **Genesis 3:24** — Flaming sword at Eden: passionate protection of the sacred. **Genesis 15:17** — God alone passes through the covenant pieces as fire: unconditional, irrevocable love. **Exodus 3:2** — The burning bush: fire that illuminates without destroying. **Exodus 13:21** — Pillar of fire for 40 years: 14,600 nights without darkness.
- Body: **1 Kings 18:38** — Fire falls on Elijah's water-soaked altar: passion not diminished by impossibility. **Isaiah 6:6** — Coal from the altar touches Isaiah's lips: the fire that undoes you is the same fire that commissions you. **Acts 2:3** — Tongues of fire rest on each believer: the democratization of divine fire.
- Takeaway: Every time God shows up as fire, the pattern is the same — He consumes what is false and illuminates what is true.

**Right column — Key verses:**
- Genesis 15:17–18: "A smoking fire pot and a flaming torch passed between these pieces. On that day the LORD made a covenant with Abram." — God walked through alone. He took the full oath upon Himself.
- Isaiah 6:7–8: "Your guilt is taken away, and your sin atoned for… Here I am! Send me." — Purification by fire always produces availability.
- Acts 2:3–4: "Divided tongues as of fire appeared to them and rested on each one of them." — Each person. Not the group. Individual fire.

## Slide 4 — "The Fire in His Eyes Is Looking for a Fire in Yours"

**Type:** Two-column content slide — closing/commission
**Eyebrow:** Passion · The Consummation
**Title:** The Fire in His Eyes Is Looking for a Fire in Yours

**Left column — The Story:**
- Section label: The Return
- Story title: Revelation 19 — The King Comes Back with Fire in His Eyes
- Body: John falls as if dead when he sees the risen Christ. The description is not gentle: eyes like a flame of fire, feet like burnished bronze refined in a furnace, voice like the roar of many waters. This is not the suffering servant of Isaiah 53. This is the Warrior-King.
- Body: The fire in His eyes is the same fire that has been burning throughout all of Scripture. It never went out. The burning bush, the pillar of fire, the coal from the altar, the tongues at Pentecost — all of it is the same fire, moving toward this moment.
- Body: Jeremiah 20:9 is the most honest verse in the Bible about passion: "There is in my heart as it were a burning fire shut up in my bones, and I am weary of holding it in, and I cannot." Jeremiah tried to quit. He could not. The fire was not his to control.
- Takeaway: The question the whole Bible asks is: when those eyes find you, will they find a fire burning in return?

**Right column — Key verses:**
- Revelation 1:14: "His eyes were like a flame of fire, his feet were like burnished bronze, refined in a furnace." — The same furnace that Shadrach walked through. The same fire that burned on Carmel.
- Jeremiah 20:9: "There is in my heart as it were a burning fire shut up in my bones, and I am weary of holding it in, and I cannot." — The most honest statement about divine passion in Scripture.
- 2 Timothy 1:6: "Fan into flame the gift of God, which is in you." — *Anazopureo* — to stir up dying embers. The fire is already in you. The question is whether you are feeding it.
