The Fire in His Eyes Is Looking for a Fire in Yours

The Return
Revelation 19 — The King Comes Back with Fire in His Eyes
John falls as if dead when he sees the risen Christ. The description is not gentle: eyes like a flame of fire, feet like burnished bronze refined in a furnace, voice like the roar of many waters. This is not the suffering servant of Isaiah 53. This is the Warrior-King.
The fire in His eyes is the same fire that has been burning throughout all of Scripture. The burning bush, the pillar of fire, the coal from the altar, the tongues at Pentecost — all of it is the same fire, moving toward this moment.
Jeremiah 20:9 is the most honest verse about passion: “There is in my heart as it were a burning fire shut up in my bones, and I am weary of holding it in, and I cannot.” Jeremiah tried to quit. He could not. The fire was not his to control.
The question the whole Bible asks: When those eyes find you, will they find a fire burning in return?
Revelation 1:14 (ESV)
“His eyes were like a flame of fire, his feet were like burnished bronze, refined in a furnace.”
The same furnace Shadrach walked through. The same fire that burned on Carmel. The fire has never gone out — it has a face now.
Jeremiah 20:9 (ESV)
“There is in my heart as it were a burning fire shut up in my bones, and I am weary of holding it in, and I cannot.”
The most honest statement about divine passion in Scripture. Jeremiah tried to quit. The fire was not his to control. Divine passion is placed, not generated.
2 Timothy 1:6 (ESV)
“Fan into flame the gift of God, which is in you.”
Anazōpureō — to stir up dying embers. The fire is already in you. The question is whether you are feeding it. Neglect it and it dims. Feed it and it blazes.
What would it look like for you to stop holding the fire in?
