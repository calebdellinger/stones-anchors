Passion · The Language of Fire

Passion Begins with a Definition, Not a Feeling

Hebrew
Qin’ah
Jealousy · Zeal
“I the LORD your God am a jealous God.”
Exodus 20:5 · ESV
Burning exclusive devotion — a fire that will not share what it loves. Also used of Phinehas’s zeal (Num 25:11) and the “zeal of the LORD of hosts” (Isa 9:7).
Greek
Pathos
Suffering · The Passion
“He presented himself alive to them after his suffering.”
Acts 1:3 · ESV
From pascho — to suffer. The Passion of Christ is the supreme definition: love so intense it absorbs the full cost of what it loves. Passion is not emotion. It is cost.
Greek
Zeō
To Boil · Fervent
“Be fervent in spirit, serve the Lord.”
Romans 12:11 · ESV
Literally, to boil. Not warm enthusiasm — a rolling, overflowing intensity. Paul commands it. Same root used of Apollos (Acts 18:25) — a man whose passion was visible to all.
Hebrew
Esh
Fire
“For the LORD your God is a consuming fire.”
Deuteronomy 4:24 · ESV
Used 370+ times in the OT. Describes God’s presence, judgment, purification, and love. Quoted in Hebrews 12:29 — the consuming fire is not an OT concept. It is eternal.
The question is not “How passionate am I?” — The question is: What am I willing to burn for?
