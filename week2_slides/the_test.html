The Ultimate Test — Genesis 22

The Story
Abraham & Isaac on Mount Moriah
God asks Abraham to sacrifice the very promise He had given him — his son Isaac, the child of covenant. This is not a test of Abraham's willingness to kill. It is a test of whether Abraham trusts the Promiser more than the promise.
Abraham's response is not argument or delay. He rises early the next morning, saddles his donkey, and goes. Three days of walking toward the altar — three days to turn back. He never does.
This is the anatomy of obedience: hearing clearly, moving immediately, and trusting completely — even when the command costs everything.
The question obedience always asks: Do you trust God more than you trust what God has given you?
Genesis 22:1–2 (ESV)
"God tested Abraham… 'Take your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to the land of Moriah. Offer him there as a burnt offering.'"
Hebrew nissah — to test, to prove. God is not trying to trip Abraham; He is revealing what is already in him.
Genesis 22:3 (ESV)
"So Abraham rose early in the morning, saddled his donkey, and took two of his young men with him, and his son Isaac."
No hesitation recorded. No negotiation. The speed of his obedience reveals the depth of his trust.
Genesis 22:18 (ESV)
"And in your offspring shall all the nations of the earth be blessed, because you have obeyed my voice."
The blessing is tied directly to the obedience. God's response to Abraham's yes is a covenant that reshapes all of human history.
