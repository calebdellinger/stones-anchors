Its Fingerprints On Civilization

~1200 BC – 90 AD
Written across 1,500 years by 40+ authors — kings, shepherds, fishermen, physicians — on three continents, in three languages, maintaining a single unified narrative thread.
~600 AD – 1400 AD
Monastic scribes preserve and copy it by hand through the collapse of Rome and the Dark Ages. It becomes the primary educational text of Western Europe.
1455 AD
Gutenberg selects the Bible as the first book printed on the movable-type press — effectively launching the information age. Mass literacy begins with this text.
1600s – 1800s
Missionaries use the Bible as the primary tool to create written languages for over 3,000 oral language groups. It becomes the founding document of global literacy infrastructure.
Present Day
The foundational text of Western law, ethics, and governance. Referenced in more legal systems, more national constitutions, and more founding documents than any other single source in history.
